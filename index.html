Godwin's Laws of Discount

A Vast Mathematical Framework for Economic Price Calculation

1. Godwin's First Law of Discount

This law dictates the precise method for calculating the amount removed from a fixed price. It states that the discount of a particular object is strictly equal to the division of the rate and the percentage constant, subsequently multiplied by the Market Price.

D = Discount Amount MP = Market Price (Fixed) d = Discount Rate (%) C = Constant (100)
D = MP * ( d / C )
Detailed Derivation:
To derive the Discount Amount, we first establish the fractional relationship between the Rate (d) and the Whole Constant (C). This yields a decimal multiplier. By applying this multiplier to the total Market Price (MP), we isolate the specific value of the discount.

2. Godwin's Second Law of Discount

The Second Law defines the Net Price—the actual value remaining after a discount has been applied. It states that the Discounted Price is the mathematical difference between the initial Market Price and the calculated Discount.

NP = MP - D
The Unified Mathematical Derivation:
By substituting the first law into the second, we create a unified formula that allows for the calculation of the final price in a single step:

Step 1: Start with NP = MP - D
Step 2: Replace D with [MP * (d/C)]
Final Unified Form: NP = (MP * C - MP * d) / C

3. Laws of Percentages on Fractions

The percentage of any value is the product of that specific number and the percentage constant. This provides the framework for converting between whole values and fractional percentage representations.

P = n * C
The Reversion Process:
To convert a percentage back into a standard numerical value, the formula is inverted. The number is equal to the product of the Percentage (P) and the inverse of the Constant (1/C).

n = P * ( 1 / C )
