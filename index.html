Godwin's Laws of Discount & Percentage

A Mathematical Framework for Price Reduction and Fractional Relationships

1. Godwin's First Law of Discount

The discount of a particular object is equal to the division of the rate ($r$) and the percentage constant ($k$) multiplied by the Fixed Price ($q$).

d = q * (r / k)

Where: d = Discount, q = Fixed Price, k = Constant (100%), r = Rate

2. Godwin's Second Law of Discount

The discounted price ($G$) is the difference between the Fixed Price ($q$) and the calculated discount ($d$).

G = q - d

By substitution, we find the unified formula for the final price:

G = (q * k - q * r) / k

3. Laws of Percentages

The percentage ($P$) of any number ($n$) is equal to the product of that number and the percentage constant ($k$). To revert to a normal number, multiply by the inverse of the constant.

P = n * k   |   n = P * (1/k)
